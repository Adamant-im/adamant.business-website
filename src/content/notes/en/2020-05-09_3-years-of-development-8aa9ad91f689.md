ADAMANT began development in 2017, and over three years the project has grown into a multi-feature decentralized platform. At its core, ADAMANT is a blockchain-based messenger paired with an integrated cryptocurrency wallet, allowing users to transfer cryptocurrencies directly within chat conversations. The platform also supports operation over the Tor network for enhanced privacy, a built-in cryptocurrency exchanger, and bot support for automated interactions.

Beyond messaging, the project has implemented two-factor authentication on a blockchain — reportedly the first such application — and introduced a private blockchain solution aimed at business use cases.

Decentralized messaging remains a long-term engineering challenge, and the ADAMANT team acknowledges that further development time is needed. A broader discussion of the timeline and technical hurdles is available in their article [Decentralized messaging needs a time](https://medium.com/adamant-im/decentralized-messaging-needs-a-time-d5af2289041c).

Despite the remaining work, the messenger is functional and in active use, with a growing audience and continued improvements. The project maintains a public [roadmap](https://adamant.im/roadmap/) outlining planned features and development milestones.
