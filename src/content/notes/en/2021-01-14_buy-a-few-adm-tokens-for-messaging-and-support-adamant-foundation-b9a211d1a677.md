ADAMANT Messenger is an open-source, blockchain-based messaging platform developed by the ADAMANT Foundation. Operating since 2017, it offers applications for Web, iOS, Tor, Windows, Linux, and macOS. The platform is designed to be completely independent of governments and corporations, making censorship impossible. It does not require phone numbers or emails, nor does it access user contact lists or geotags. User IP addresses are hidden from other participants, and a dedicated Tor application is available for users requiring heightened anonymity.

Security is enforced through robust cryptographic standards. ADAMANT utilizes Diffie-Hellman Curve25519 for key exchange, Salsa20 and Poly1305 for encryption, and SHA-256 with Ed25519 EdDSA for digital signatures. Users retain full control of their private keys at all times.

Beyond standard messaging, ADAMANT integrates cryptocurrency functionality directly within chats. Users can store and transfer digital assets in-chat, utilize an internal exchanger, and leverage a blockchain-based two-factor authentication (2FA) solution. The platform also offers a specialized ADAMANT for Business solution.

Network operations require small fees paid in ADM tokens. Acquiring ADM also supports the ongoing development of the ADAMANT Foundation. The foundation accepts various cryptocurrencies for token acquisition, including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Litecoin, Dash, Lisk, and Dogecoin.
