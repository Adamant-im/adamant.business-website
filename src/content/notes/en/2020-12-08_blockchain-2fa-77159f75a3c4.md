ADAMANT's two-factor authentication mechanism leverages blockchain technology to provide a verification layer that differs fundamentally from conventional 2FA approaches. Traditional 2FA methods—SMS codes, authenticator apps, or hardware tokens—rely on centralized infrastructure and are susceptible to interception, phishing, or service outages. By moving the second factor onto a blockchain, ADAMANT eliminates dependence on a single provider and creates a verifiable, tamper-resistant authentication channel.

To make this capability accessible beyond the ADAMANT messaging platform itself, the project now operates a dedicated service at [blockchain2fa.io](https://blockchain2fa.io). This separates the 2FA functionality into its own product, allowing third-party applications and services to integrate blockchain-based verification without adopting the full ADAMANT client.

![Blockchain 2FA](/images/engineering-notes/medium/77159f75a3c4/002-0-cztc2u-vz5iu6hzl.webp)

The underlying mechanism uses on-chain transactions as the second factor: a verification request triggers a blockchain transaction that the user must authorize from their ADAMANT wallet. Because the transaction is cryptographically signed and recorded on a public ledger, neither the requesting service nor an intermediary can forge or replay the authentication step. This approach is particularly relevant for high-value operations where the integrity of the second factor is critical.
