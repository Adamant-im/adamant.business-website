Version 1.3.0 of the ADAMANT exchanger adds support for BZ, the native token of the Bit-Z cryptocurrency exchange. Bit-Z, founded in 2016 in the British Virgin Islands, operates branches across 10 countries and regions and is ranked fourth globally by the assessment agency Tokeninsight.

![Buy and sell Bit-Z Token (BZ) in the ADAMANT exchanger](/images/engineering-notes/medium/2bd7b5e2ecd/002-1-stvh-rxexfmq69tanmwla-png.webp)

The ADAMANT exchanger now supports BZ, ETH, USDS, RES, and ADM. To make an exchange, send the cryptocurrency you want to spend as an in-chat payment to the exchanger and include the ticker of the coin you want to receive in the comment field. For example, to exchange 0.05 ETH for BZ, send an in-chat payment of 0.05 ETH to the exchanger with the comment "BZ."

![Buy and sell Bit-Z Token (BZ) in the ADAMANT exchanger](/images/engineering-notes/medium/2bd7b5e2ecd/003-1-cuie-e-mzj8hbbr3bbo6ya-png.webp)

This release also improves performance through WebSocket connection support. Users can run their own exchanger instance following the open-source documentation, and third-party exchangers are supported as well, though users should only trust operators they can verify.
