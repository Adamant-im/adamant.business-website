ADAMANT's market-making bot (adamant-tradebot) is a free, self-hosted market-making bot for cryptocurrency projects and token issuers. It manages volume, spread, liquidity, price ranges, and dynamic order books from a single application.

## Editions

The bot ships in two versions. The public, free, open-source edition is suitable for small crypto projects with low liquidity and supports a limited list of centralized exchanges. The paid premium edition adds advanced features and broader exchange support. You can start with the free version immediately; the premium version is available through the marketmaking.app website.

## Target Users

The bot is primarily intended for cryptocurrency owners and issuers who have created their own token, listed it on an exchange, and want to maintain healthy-looking markets. Good market-making attracts traders and investors. Crypto exchanges can also benefit, either by running the bot directly on desired trading pairs or by incentivizing listed projects to run their own bots to provide more liquidity and keep order books filled.

## Free Version Features

The free edition is self-hosted and self-controlled, meaning you retain full custody of your exchange API keys and balances. It builds dynamic order books and supports four market-making policies: spread, order book, optimal, and depth. You can maintain spread and liquidity, set price ranges, and perform arbitrage by watching token prices on other trading pairs and exchanges.

## Advantages

Compared to competing solutions, the bot charges no monthly fees or subscriptions. Because it is self-hosted, your exchange API keys never leave your server, and there is no need to transfer BTC, USDT, or tokens to any third party. The bot is self-managed, allowing you to update market-making parameters at any time while it runs.

## Getting Started

Install the bot from the GitHub repository on your server or VPS and configure it. Once set up, you can customize trading parameters such as trading amounts, liquidity, and order book settings. The bot is managed through text commands via ADAMANT Messenger.
