The ADAMANT blockchain has seen measurable growth across its infrastructure, strengthening the decentralized foundation that supports its privacy-focused messaging platform.

### Nodes and Delegates

According to the network's monitors, the ADAMANT blockchain now operates with 109 nodes, providing a distributed framework that underpins its decentralized nature. This expansion is important for network resilience and performance, helping ensure transactions remain secure and reliable.

![ADAMANT network grows](/images/engineering-notes/medium/008f91b6d9e6/002-1-eza2nvitsduko9xgpou1og-png.webp)

The network has also reached 319 delegates. These delegates participate in the consensus mechanism and block validation process, making them central to how ADAMANT maintains its ledger.

### New Forging Pool: aliens_pool

![ADAMANT network grows](/images/engineering-notes/medium/008f91b6d9e6/003-0-dcbbxix2bjwnddyo.webp)

A new forging pool called aliens_pool has been introduced to the community. It offers a reward sharing scheme that distributes 80% of its rewards every Sunday, with a minimum payout threshold of 10 ADM. This model is intended to incentivize community participation and support for the network's forging process.

The addition of new forging pools gives community members more options for earning rewards while also contributing to the decentralization of the ADAMANT network. Decentralization remains a core principle of the project, ensuring no single entity controls the network and promoting transparency and security.

For cryptofoundry readers interested in the technical details of how ADAMANT pools and delegate voting work, the open-source ADAMANT project provides documentation on the consensus and rewards process.
