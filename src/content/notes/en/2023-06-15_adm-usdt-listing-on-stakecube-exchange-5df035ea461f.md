ADAMANT, the open-source secure messaging platform, has listed its native token ADM on StakeCube Exchange. The ADM/USDT trading pair provides a direct way to trade ADM against a US dollar-pegged stablecoin, improving accessibility and liquidity for the token.

This listing is part of a long-term cooperation plan between ADAMANT and StakeCube. The partnership has previously included integration support for the ADAMANT market-making bot on StakeCube, making the exchange a supported venue for automated ADM trading.

### Trading Pair Details

The ADM/USDT pair allows users to buy, sell, and exchange ADM without intermediate conversions through other cryptocurrencies. Because USDT is pegged to the US dollar, the pair gives traders a stable reference price and reduces exposure to volatility from crypto-to-crypto pairs. Direct fiat-pegged trading also lowers transaction costs by eliminating extra conversion steps.

StakeCube is an established exchange offering a range of trading pairs and security measures. Listing ADM there exposes the token to a broader audience of traders and investors who may not have previously interacted with the ADAMANT ecosystem.

### Implications for ADAMANT

Greater exchange availability supports ADAMANT's broader adoption by making the token easier to acquire. As more users gain access to ADM, they can participate in the platform's messaging economy, including in-chat transfers and bot interactions that rely on ADM as a transactional medium. Increased trading activity can also contribute to price discovery and market depth.

The partnership with StakeCube reflects ADAMANT's strategy of expanding token availability across exchanges that support its market-making and trading infrastructure. Users interested in automated trading can refer to the ADAMANT market-making bot documentation for StakeCube integration details.
