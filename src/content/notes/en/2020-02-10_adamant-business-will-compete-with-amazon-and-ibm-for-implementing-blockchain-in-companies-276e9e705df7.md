Distributed Ledger Technology and blockchain are increasingly adopted by companies for business optimization and cost reduction. Major providers such as Amazon and IBM already offer enterprise blockchain services, alongside platforms like Hyperledger, Ethereum Enterprise, Corda, Quorum, and MultiChain. These solutions, however, are geared toward large enterprises and compete primarily on performance, transactions per second, and scalability.

Small and medium-sized companies have different requirements. They typically need only a handful of transactions per minute, not millions per second, and what matters most is a clear answer to what blockchain will actually give them and how to start using it in practice.

ADAMANT has demonstrated a working blockchain messenger over three years of development. The public ADAMANT blockchain served as a proof of concept for private corporate blockchains built on the same foundation. ADAMANT Business is that private blockchain, designed for small and medium-sized organizations. Unlike the public network, all ADAMANT Business data is stored on the company's own nodes, giving the company full data ownership — including the ability to completely destroy data or roll back to a previous point. This approach offers what can be described as "centralized decentralization."

ADAMANT Business provides a blockchain, a company-internal token, and a platform for secure blockchain-based messaging. Unlike competing enterprise solutions, it is free, easy to install, and straightforward to maintain. Enterprise platforms such as Hyperledger are notoriously complex to use, often requiring expensive consultants and months of effort just to run a trial. ADAMANT Business can be installed by an ordinary IT specialist in a few days.

The internal token can be used to exchange for goods and services, build a reward system, track working hours, or support other internal workflows. It lets a company or community experiment with a cryptocurrency-like mechanism without costs or risks to its core operations; if something goes wrong, there is nothing to lose.
