*19 June 2025*

Since the first lines of code were written in 2017, ADAMANT has sought to prove that everyday messaging, payments, and data exchange can be friction-free *and* sovereign. Eight years later, the project’s roadmap spans mature products, an expanding ecosystem, and a newly community-driven governance model. This is where ADAMANT stands today and where it is headed next.

See the updated Roadmap: [https://adamant.im/roadmap](https://adamant.im/roadmap/)

By Q1 2024, ADAMANT had evolved from a single-purpose blockchain messenger into a battle-tested, multi-asset communications stack with native wallets, exchange tools, and business-grade automation.

Since conception in 2017, the mission has been rooted in privacy, transparency, and technological independence. Mainnet launched in Q4 2017, and many groundbreaking features followed: in-chat crypto transactions for multiple currencies (BTC, ETH, DOGE, DASH, LSK, BNB, and more), messenger and wallet apps for iOS, Android, PWA, and desktop, blockchain 2FA, market-making bots, decentralized services, Tor and anonymous access support, plus console tools, API libraries, and full-node management.

## Q2 2025 → Q1 2026: What Comes Next

By Q1 2026, users will have always-reachable, censorship-resistant messengers backed by faster nodes, protocol-level anti-spam, and first-class cold-storage support — all steered by an open community. More importantly, the target is free, more private messaging over existing blockchain bandwidth.

**Q2 2025** focuses on censorship-resistant delivery and performance: partnership with Massa Chain for DeWeb hosting, WebSocket node-to-node protocol (Node 0.10), split-screen PWA 4.10, iOS 3.11 UX tweaks, first testnet PWAs, and extra download mirrors on GitHub and adm.im.

![ADAMANT Roadmap Update: Steady Progress, Fresh Momentum](/images/engineering-notes/medium/404ec5b95889/017-1-b-ygemeozphoed-gcnsm5q-png.webp)

Upcoming split-screen view in ADAMANT Messenger.

**Q3 2025** emphasizes reliability and automation: push notifications for PWA, local-storage fast login on iOS, the ADAMANT Notification Service (ANS), reproducible builds auto-deployed to Massa DeWeb, adm.im, and Google Play, Forging Pool 3.1, ADM Airdrop 3.0, the Currencyinfo.dev portal for developers, and groundwork for business integrations.

**Q4 2025** brings freedom-first messaging: free, more private messaging over existing blockchain bandwidth, secret wallets and biometric login for PWA/iOS, WalletConnect on iOS, Explorer 2.0, anti-spam at the protocol level, and AIP-20 to introduce group chats and open the social layer.

**Q1 2026** delivers cold-grade security and a fee overhaul: offline signing and cold-wallet mode for PWA, WalletConnect in PWA, AIP-19 to modernize fee calculation and cut delegate costs, on-chain group chat/channel prototypes, and a dedicated wallet website.

## From Q2 2026 Onward: Vision & Stretch Goals (2026–2027)

Long-range possibilities, subject to community votes and funding, include deletable and pinnable chats, an ADAMANT Mixer for on-chain obfuscation, a secure note/password vault, a Bot Factory API, and a Tor-native site. Further out: Random Chat rollout, global advertising and sponsorship push, renewed pool promotions, deeper enterprise pilots, ADM on top-tier centralized exchanges, human-readable “ADAMANT Naming” addresses, a multi-account UI, blockchain-signed document vaults, voice calling, and a third-party security audit to certify the full stack.

## The Road Ahead

The past year underscored ADAMANT’s capacity to ship complex features — IPFS transfers, WebSocket networking, self-healing health checks — while reinventing its governance. The upcoming four quarters focus on censorship resistance, flawless reliability, and frictionless onboarding. Beyond that, the roadmap sketches ambitious but attainable innovations in privacy, identity, and real-time communication.

ADAMANT continues to evolve, with an increasingly engaged community steering the helm — turning the “possible” column of its roadmap into everyday features, one quarter at a time.
