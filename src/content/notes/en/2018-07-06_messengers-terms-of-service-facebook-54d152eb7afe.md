This article begins a series examining the most consequential parts of user agreements from major messaging services—agreements that most users never read. A significant portion of users' lives is transmitted through these networks, and understanding how that data is handled is essential. First on the list is Facebook, the largest social network and a foundational layer of digital life for billions of people worldwide.

The information discussed here applies to all Facebook products, including Facebook, Instagram, Facebook Messenger, WhatsApp, and other attached services. This ecosystem covers more than 2.5 billion people, with up to 85% of users in the 14–24 age range in the US, Germany, and France.

It is worth noting that corporate user agreements are often drafted with deliberately imprecise terms, complex sentence structures, and other legal-documentation techniques. These practices give corporations significant advantages in scandals, lawsuits, and regulatory matters while simultaneously making the text difficult for ordinary readers to interpret. Each ambiguity is calculated in advance.

The excerpts below are taken directly from Facebook's official privacy policy without altering the wording.

### Data Collection

Facebook states that it collects content, messages, and other information—both from content you provide and information about it. The company collects data about what you see through the functions it provides, as well as information about the people you are associated with and your interactions with them across Facebook products. If you download, synchronize, or import contact information from your device, that data is collected too.

Beyond direct content, Facebook collects information about the types of content you view or interact with, the actions you take, the people you interact with, and the time, frequency, and duration of your activity. It also collects information about how you use features such as the camera. If you use Facebook products for purchases or other financial transactions, that transaction data is collected. Notably, Facebook also receives and analyzes content, messages, and information that *other people* provide about you. Additionally, it collects information from the devices you use that are connected to the internet.

### Sharing With Third Parties

Facebook provides access to your information, stores it, and shares it with regulatory and law enforcement authorities and others. The company may access or retain information about you for a longer period if it is the subject of an official request, legal obligation, government investigation, investigation into possible violations of terms and conditions, or other cases.

### Data Retention

Facebook retains information from accounts disabled for breach of conditions for at least a year in order to prevent repeated violations. Otherwise, retention periods are determined on an individual basis—a deliberately flexible standard that leaves users with little clarity about how long their data persists.

### Commentary

According to Facebook, the primary reasons for data collection are improving news feeds and selecting content based on interests and business advertising. Given the breadth of the permissions outlined above, cases where the microphone appeared to be used for data collection by the application remain entirely plausible and, based on the information provided, fully compliant with EU and US laws.

Most Facebook press releases use vague language and avoid clear answers to media questions. Readers are encouraged to review the original source text and draw their own conclusions about the implications of these policies.
