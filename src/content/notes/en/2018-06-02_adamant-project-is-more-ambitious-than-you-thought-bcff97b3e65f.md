The ADAMANT open-source project has developed a messenger with instant private messaging and ADM token transfers. The development team is now focused on extending the platform to support multi-token transfers within private chats, with the goal of allowing cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Lisk to be sent and received directly through the messenger.

The ADM token underpins the platform's economics. It serves as the transfer fee for messaging and payments, maintaining the network infrastructure. With a 5-second block time, ADM enables fast transaction speeds and is accessible directly from chat screens for payments. ADM operates as a fully independent, self-sufficient token system.

![ADAMANT Project is more ambitious than you thought.](/images/engineering-notes/medium/bcff97b3e65f/002-0-8ofcnhmzjqjcc-p8.webp)

Incorporating major cryptocurrencies into the transfer ecosystem is intended to attract users from those communities to the ADAMANT Messenger platform, generating higher fees and greater demand for ADM.

## Privacy Architecture

ADAMANT is a secure blockchain platform for data and message transfers built on a Lisk codebase. The blockchain provides anonymity that prevents a user's chat history from being traced. Unlike centralized social networks that collect and store personal data on third-party servers vulnerable to breaches, ADAMANT requires only a private key to use the system, which can be generated with a single click.

Users do not need to provide names, email addresses, phone numbers, or device information. The open-source codebase allows anyone to audit the system's authenticity and build independent implementations.

Key privacy features include no access to user address books or location information, complete anonymity with no user identifications, and end-to-end encryption where messages are encrypted on the sender's device and decrypted on the recipient's side. The client app performs all cryptographic operations locally and never transmits the private key or mnemonic phrase over the network. Message history is loaded directly from the blockchain rather than stored locally, and unlike P2P messengers, user IP addresses cannot be obtained. ADAMANT accounts cannot be closed, blocked, or limited by anyone, including the developers.

ADAMANT demonstrates that blockchain messengers offer advantages in openness, message protection, distribution, and reliable infrastructure, providing anonymous messaging without third-party interruption or regulation.
