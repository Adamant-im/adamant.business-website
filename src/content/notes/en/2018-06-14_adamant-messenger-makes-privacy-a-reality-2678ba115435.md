ADAMANT Messenger is a web-based messaging service that uses blockchain encryption and decryption to keep conversations private. Messages are fully encrypted on the sender's device and decrypted on the recipient's device, meaning no intermediary ever sees plaintext content. Message history is never stored on a local device; it is loaded directly from the blockchain on demand.

Because all cryptographic work happens locally, the client application is incapable of transferring a user's private key or mnemonic phrase over the network. This means user identity remains secure, IP addresses are masked, and location is untraceable. ADAMANT accounts cannot be closed, blocked, or limited by anyone, including the project's developers.

The service is fee-based, using low-cost ADM tokens that can be funded through the built-in anonymous wallet. Costs are minimal and are expected to decrease as the network grows. New accounts receive enough ADM to begin sending messages immediately at no charge. The wallet can be funded with several digital currencies, including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Dash, and Litecoin.

There is nothing to install—ADAMANT runs in any web browser. Access it from any internet-connected device by entering your secure mnemonic phrase.

To get started, visit the ADAMANT web client and click "Create new." Copy the generated mnemonic phrase to a secure location, as it is the only credential needed to access your account. Enter the phrase to log in, and begin chatting with another ADAMANT user.
