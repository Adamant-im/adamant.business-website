Most "enterprise blockchain" projects die between the slide deck and the first deployment, because the platform demands a consultancy to operate. ADAMANT is the opposite case: a blockchain messenger that has run in public production since 2017, installable and maintainable with ordinary IT skills. We are its engineers, and we deploy it privately for organizations that need communication they fully control.

## What your organization actually gets

A messenger your staff already knows how to use — chats, groups, transfers — running on nodes your IT department operates. Every message is end-to-end encrypted and stored on your chain, not on someone else's cloud. You decide retention, access, and recovery policy, and you can audit the entire stack: it is open source, top to bottom.

## Scenarios we deploy

**Internal communication that leaves no third party in the loop.** For companies whose conversations are commercially or legally sensitive, the value is structural: there is no vendor to subpoena, breach, or acquire. The infrastructure is yours.

**An internal token economy.** ADAMANT ships with in-chat transfers and multi-chain wallets. Organizations use this for settlements between departments, partner rewards, or gated access — an internal unit of account that moves inside the same tool people already chat in, with no enterprise-integration diagrams.

**Blockchain-delivered 2FA and notifications.** Codes and alerts delivered through the chain instead of SMS — immune to SIM swaps, verifiable, and self-hosted. A practical first project that proves the stack without betting the company on it.

**Bots and workflow automation in-chat.** Support queues, approval flows, payout bots — the automation patterns from our bots practice, running inside your private messenger.

## Two deployment shapes

Join the public ADAMANT network when you want encrypted messaging with minimal operations overhead — your nodes, shared chain. Run a private fork when the chain itself must be yours: your validators, your token, your rules. We help you pick honestly; a private fork is more control and more responsibility, and it is not the right default for everyone.
